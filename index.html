Senior Thesis

Natural Language Processing in Semi-Structured Text

This project uses natural language processing techniques to determine the language of files of data records. The files contain names and addresses of businesses, parks, works of art, and similar; these files are not assumed to have any grammatical structure, or general formatting for the data. Several publicly available data tests are utilized in this project for the testing and training of the techniques and algorithms used in the solving of the problem. The methods implemented and tested were the Naive Bayes algorithm, Markov models, and k-nearest neighbors.

Airplane Boarding

The Airplane Seating Problem

It is up to each individual airline to determine the best process to complete the boarding procedure. There is no guaranteed consistency across airlines; however, most allow passengers with special needs and those seated in first class to board first, followed by the rest of the passengers. Major airlines board the rest of the passengers by grouped rows, first boarding those passengers seated in the back of the aircraft and then progressing forward. Southwest, however, does not assign seats. Passengers are boarded according to when they arrived at the airport, choosing a seat as they board. With the development of larger planes, such as the Airbus A380 (800 passengers), airlines are working to minimize boarding time. An aircraft only makes money when it is moving, so long boarding times limit the number of trips a single plane can make in one day. By minimizing the time spent on the ground, specifically the time boarding and deboarding, airlines can increase the number of flights on a single day, thus increasing their revenue. We modeled and analyzed the effectiveness of each procedure, and also created our own.

In collaboration with Eli Sorey.
